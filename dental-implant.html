Dental Implants

What is a Dental Implant?

A dental implant is an artificial tooth root that is placed into your jawbone to hold a replacement tooth or bridge. It looks, feels, and works like a natural tooth. Implants are usually made of titanium, a strong and biocompatible metal that fuses well with bone.

Parts of a Dental Implant

  • Implant Fixture (Screw): The titanium post surgically inserted into the jawbone acting as the root.
  • Abutment: A small connector that attaches the crown to the implant fixture.
  • Crown: The visible part of the tooth — made of ceramic or porcelain — that looks like a real tooth.

Procedure (Step-by-Step)

  1. Initial Examination & X-ray: Dentist checks bone condition, gum health, and takes scans.
  2. Tooth Extraction (if needed): Damaged or decayed tooth is removed.
  3. Implant Placement: The titanium screw is surgically placed into the jawbone.
  4. Healing Period (Osseointegration): Bone grows around the implant — this takes 3 to 6 months.
  5. Abutment Placement: After healing, the abutment is attached.
  6. Crown Placement: A custom-made crown is placed on top to complete the implant.

Time Required

The full process may take 3 to 9 months, depending on bone healing speed, number of implants, and implant type.

Cost (Approximate)

India: ₹25,000 – ₹60,000 per implant

USA: $1,500 – $6,000 per implant

(Costs depend on material, clinic, and dentist’s experience.)

Advantages of Dental Implants

  • Look and feel like natural teeth
  • Prevent bone loss in the jaw
  • Can last 15–25 years or more
  • Improves speech and chewing
  • No damage to nearby teeth (unlike bridges)

Possible Disadvantages / Risks

  • Surgery required
  • More expensive compared to dentures
  • Takes several months for healing
  • Risk of infection or implant failure (rare)

Aftercare Tips

  • Brush and floss daily
  • Avoid smoking and alcohol (delays healing)
  • Visit your dentist regularly
  • Eat soft food for a few days after surgery

Types of Dental Implants

  • Endosteal Implants: Placed directly into the jawbone (most common).
  • Subperiosteal Implants: Placed under the gum but above the jawbone (for low bone density patients).
  • Zygomatic Implants: Used when jawbone is weak; anchored in the cheekbone.
