Dentures

What Are Dentures?

Dentures are removable artificial teeth used to replace missing natural teeth and surrounding tissues. They help restore your smile, speech, and chewing ability when natural teeth are lost due to decay, injury, or age. Dentures are usually made from acrylic resin, sometimes combined with metal or porcelain for added strength and a natural look.

Types of Dentures

  1. Complete (Full) Dentures: Used when all teeth in the upper or lower jaw are missing.
    • Immediate Dentures: Placed right after tooth extraction (temporary).
    • Conventional Dentures: Placed after gums heal (usually after 6–8 weeks).
  2. Partial Dentures: Used when some natural teeth remain; they fill the gaps using a metal or acrylic framework that clips onto remaining teeth.
  3. Implant-Supported Dentures: Attach to dental implants placed in the jawbone for stronger stability and grip.
  4. Flexible Dentures: Made of soft, flexible resin instead of hard acrylic — lightweight and comfortable.

Procedure (Step-by-Step)

  1. Dental Examination: The dentist checks gums, jawbone, and existing teeth.
  2. Impressions (Molds): Measurements of your mouth are taken to design custom dentures.
  3. Tooth Extraction (if needed): Damaged teeth are removed before denture fitting.
  4. Try-in Phase: A wax model of the dentures is tested for fit and comfort.
  5. Final Denture Placement: The finished dentures are fitted and adjusted.
  6. Follow-up Visits: Minor adjustments made to improve comfort.

Cost (Approximate)

India: ₹10,000 – ₹60,000 (per arch) — depends on material and denture type.

USA: $600 – $8,000 (per arch).

Advantages of Dentures

  • Restore your smile and confidence
  • Improve chewing and speech
  • Support facial muscles and shape
  • Non-surgical and affordable
  • Custom-made for your mouth

Disadvantages / Limitations

  • Can feel uncomfortable at first
  • May move or slip while eating/talking
  • Requires regular cleaning and maintenance
  • Doesn’t stop bone loss in the jaw
  • May need replacement every 5–10 years

Care & Maintenance Tips

  • Rinse dentures after eating
  • Brush them daily with a soft denture brush (no toothpaste)
  • Soak them overnight in a denture cleaning solution
  • Brush gums and tongue before wearing dentures
  • Handle carefully — avoid dropping them
  • Visit your dentist every 6–12 months for check-ups

Lifespan

With good care, dentures usually last 5 to 10 years before needing replacement or relining.

When Should You Get Dentures?

You may need dentures if you have missing several or all teeth, difficulty chewing or speaking, sagging facial appearance, or gum irritation/tooth loss due to decay or injury.
