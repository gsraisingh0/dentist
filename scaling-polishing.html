Scaling & Polishing

What is Scaling and Polishing?

Scaling and Polishing is a professional dental cleaning procedure done by a dentist or dental hygienist to remove plaque, tartar (calculus), and stains from your teeth and to make them smooth and shiny. It is one of the most common and important preventive dental treatments to keep your teeth and gums healthy.

1. What is Scaling?

Scaling means removing hard deposits (tartar or calculus) and soft plaque from the tooth surfaces — especially around and below the gum line.

How it is done:

  • The dentist uses a manual scaler or ultrasonic scaler.
  • The ultrasonic scaler has a vibrating metal tip and water spray that helps remove tartar easily and cools the area.
  • Scaling removes: plaque (soft, sticky bacteria film), tartar (hardened plaque), stains from food, coffee, tea, or smoking.

Purpose of Scaling: To prevent gum disease (gingivitis, periodontitis), to remove bacteria buildup, to reduce bad breath, and to improve gum health.

2. What is Polishing?

Polishing is done after scaling to make the tooth surfaces smooth and shiny.

How it is done: The dentist uses a rubber cup and a polishing paste (gritty toothpaste). It removes minor surface stains left after scaling. Polishing helps make it harder for plaque to stick to the teeth again.

3. Step-by-Step Procedure

  1. Initial Examination: Dentist checks your teeth and gums for tartar buildup and gum inflammation.
  2. Scaling: Removal of plaque and tartar using manual or ultrasonic tools.
  3. Rinsing: To remove loose debris.
  4. Polishing: Teeth are polished with a special paste to make them smooth and shiny.
  5. Fluoride (optional): Sometimes fluoride is applied to protect against cavities.

4. Time Required

Usually 30 to 45 minutes for the full mouth. Depends on how much tartar or stain you have.

5. Cost (Approximate)

India: ₹500 – ₹2000 per sitting

USA/UK: $75 – $150 per session

6. Aftercare Tips

  • Avoid tea, coffee, and smoking for 24 hours (to prevent new stains).
  • Brush twice daily using a soft-bristled brush.
  • Floss daily.
  • Use mouthwash as recommended.
  • Visit your dentist every 6 months.

7. Common Side Effects (Temporary)

  • Mild gum sensitivity or bleeding for a day or two
  • Slight tooth sensitivity to hot/cold foods — these go away quickly with good oral care.

8. Benefits of Scaling and Polishing

  • Removes harmful plaque and tartar
  • Prevents cavities and gum disease
  • Freshens breath
  • Improves smile and appearance
  • Makes teeth smooth and easier to clean
